Farm Management



48

years of experience

The site of a fruit-growing enterprise is as significant in determining its success as the varieties grown. In fact, variety and site together set a ceiling on the productivity and profit that can be realized under the best management. In fruit regions microclimatic conditions (climate at plant height, as influenced by slight differences in soil, soil covering, and elevation) and soil conditions are the two components of a site that determine its desirability for a fruit-growing enterprise. Sometimes transportation to market must also be considered.

Local conditions at a site that expose it to unusual frost hazard are as detrimental to citrus as they are to peach trees and apple trees. In regions and sites where temperatures during the season may drop no more than a few degrees below freezing, artificial frost protection is sometimes used. This is accomplished by open-flame burning or heating of metal objects with oil, gas, propane, electricity, etc. Another technique is the spraying of water on plants (e.g., strawberries) as long as the temperature is below freezing.



Harvesting and Packing



The proper time to remove a fruit from the tree or plant varies with each fruit and is governed by whether the product will be sold and consumed within hours, or stored for weeks, months, or even a year. Most fruits are harvested as close as possible to the time they are eaten. A few, of which banana and pear may be harvested while immature and still ripen satisfactorily. Orange, grapefruit, and some varieties of avocado may be stored on the tree for several months after they have attained good quality.

For the fresh market, most tree and bush fruits are still harvested by hand. For processing, drying, and occasionally for fresh market, mechanical motor-driven tree and bush shakers with appropriate catching belts, bins, pallets, and electric lifts reduce harvesting and handling labour. In years to come, machinery may make it possible to machine-harvest most fruits, with no more, and possibly less, damage than with hand picking.
